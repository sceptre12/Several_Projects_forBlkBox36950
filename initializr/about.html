Student Affairs and Student Persistence: Building Co-Curricular Programs for Student Success 10/23/2013

10:00AM - 12:00PM		 Units: 4
Tier: 2

Location: MMC: GC 243;
BBC: WUC 159
Required: Yes

Description
As higher education begins to focus more intently on connecting access initiatives to student success and graduation responsibilities, the role of the co-curricular becomes even more important. This presentation will offer student affairs practitioners several lenses through which they can begin to frame their work as part of the institutional “persistence and graduation” conversation. Learners will be introduced to Dr. Watson Swail’s model of Student Persistence and Achievement as well as a curricular approach to structuring student affairs programming efforts. The session will conclude with a few tangible examples of co-curricular programs that address student risk and student development in innovative and measurable ways.
